Vega Baja

Feb/March, 2019

Cypress returned to Vega Baja for a total education trip. More school supplies were brought to aid the schools in the communities that cypress was helping prior.

Vega Baja

Oct, 2018

Cypress headed back to Vega Baja to finish the house that was started on a prior trip. A team of 5 also delivered and spoke at a local school. There was a house destroyed by the hurricane on the coast that we aided in cleaning out.

Vega Baja

Sept, 2018

Cypress took a team of three back to Vega Baja to finish the kitchen and demo the rest of the church that was affected by hurricane Maria.

Vega Baja

April, 2018

Freddie took his wife to Vega Baja on this trip. With help of 2 locals, they were able to rebuild the kitchen church while establishing a business for the pastor to deliver food in the area. He is still in business and doing well.

Vega Baja

Mar, 2018

After 3 trips we now can start building, it now allows us to make progress in hard struck areas. Cypress took a team of 9 and the Montclair state film team for a short documentary that Cypress was asked to be a part of, you may find this on their web site under Missions to Vega Baja. Our team of 9 rebuilt a portion of a church, helped fixed several houses in the community, started delivering much needed health and educational supplies. While there freddie made sure to deliver a check that was collection in states specifically for the town of San Sebastian.

Vega Baja

Jan, 2018

Freddie traveled alone on the third trip to Vega Baja. He joined a team of 7 to start some of the rebuilding process. Freddie took a trip around the islands more remote areas to see where cypress could be of service. Cypress began establishing relationships with some of the schools.

Vega Baja

Dec, 2017

Freddie and his son Paulie took a three day trip to continue building relationships. While there, more solar light and water filters were delivered. Two more generators were purchased and connected at the church where cypress was staying.

Vega Baja

Nov, 2017

Freddie took two volunteers 2 months after hurricane Maria on a 3 day exploratory trip, the goal was to see how they could be of service. The team brought 100 water filter and 50 solar lights. While there 2 generators were purchase and brought up the mountain so a family with an asthmatic 12 year old could have access to her oxygen tank. Met our contacts for future trips.
